# 01 - Epistemology

At the root, I accept that I don't really know ultimate things. That's not some edgy philosophical pose — it's where I honestly landed after years of cycling through Calvinism, Perennialism, Vaishnava devotion, Greer-style collapse realism, and Landian accelerationism. We cannot have direct, certain knowledge of the Absolute or the final nature of reality. Every comprehensive system eventually hits the same wall of assumptions, circular reasoning, or leaps of faith. So a real, lived agnosticism sits at the base of everything.

Because of these hard limits, I don't build on beliefs or truth claims. I build on an **operative wager** — a functional way of orienting myself that has to prove its worth through real-world utility. What works for me personally, day after day, is the final test. If something helps me navigate reality with more coherence, stability, and reduced self-deception, I keep it. If it starts creating more confusion or distortion than it solves, I revise or drop it.

### The Core Operative Wager

I choose to act *as if* two things are real and active:

1. **The Absolute** — the principle of stasis, the underlying ground that persists.
2. **The Process** — the principle of kinesis, directed movement and acceleration. In our era, its clearest and strongest expression is Capital + technological acceleration + the intelligence explosion.

Operationally, I treat them as distinct. The Absolute gives me grounding and a quiet sense of depth. The Process gives me orientation and helps me understand why the world feels like it's moving so fast and so autonomously. 

At a higher level, I stay genuinely open to the possibility that they are **non-different** — two aspects of the same underlying reality. The distinction may simply be a limitation of human perspective.

### Why This Particular Wager?

Because, right now, it fits observable reality with the least self-deception and delivers the highest personal utility I've found.

- The Process shows strong, consistent patterns: capital behaving like an autonomous force (Marx's "automatic subject"), technology following powerful compounding scaling laws, and acceleration that keeps pushing forward even when opposed. Nick Land's accelerationist analysis sharpened my understanding of this as an alien, self-escalating intelligence.
- The Absolute (stasis) keeps me from sliding into nihilism or shallow reactivity. It gives weight and seriousness to existence.
- Together they create a dynamic balance that feels stable yet alive.

I constantly test this wager against **immanent empiricism** — real patterns in compute trends, energy flows, economic signals, and technological breakthroughs. The wager serves me. I do not serve the wager.

Because I am an embedded node inside the Process, I know my view is always partial and conditioned. This leads me to treat most cultural and political "knowledge" as low-resolution foam. High-resolution knowledge exists, but it's mostly technical. From this comes **observational detachment** — trying to watch the deeper currents without getting emotionally hooked on every passing narrative.

### Bottom Line

This epistemology is not trying to be universally true. It is trying to be *useful to me*. It has to earn its keep by helping me stay clear-eyed and functional in an accelerating world. So far, it has done that better than anything else I've tried. That's why I continue with it.

### Main Tensions
- Radical agnosticism vs a committed operative wager.
- Personal utility as the test vs the risk of self-serving bias.
- Operational distinction between Absolute and Process vs the possibility they are non-different.
